# MIP-X39: rETH Market Exchange Rate Feed and Debt Management

### **Summary**

This proposal recommends updating the oracle configuration for the rETH market
on both Base and OP Mainnet from the current Chainlink market price oracle to a
Chainlink exchange-rate based oracle. While the existing market price oracle has
functioned adequately under current liquidity conditions, using an exchange-rate
feed better reflects rETH’s design and improves the protocol’s long-term
resilience by reducing exposure to short-term market dislocations.

Using an exchange-rate feed more accurately reflects rETH’s design, reduces
unnecessary oracle volatility, and improves the protocol’s overall risk posture.

Furthermore, after discussion with Anthias, Lunar Labs contributors desire to
use some of the WETH reserves to pay off debt accrued to the rETH markets and
some to the cbETH markets as well. Specific details for amounts are listed
within this proposal.

### **Background**

rETH (Rocket Pool ETH) is a non-rebasing liquid staking token whose value
increases over time relative to ETH via an internal exchange rate.

The rETH market on both Base and OP Mainnet currently rely on a Chainlink market
price oracle. Although this approach is acceptable when liquidity is deep and
routing is efficient, it can introduce avoidable volatility in collateral
valuation and liquidation behavior during periods of fragmented liquidity or
transient price dislocations. As both networks continue to mature, hardening
oracle design can improve robustness under a wider range of market conditions.

As part of a broader review of Moonwell’s ETH-derivative oracles, Moonwell’s
risk advisors at Anthias noted that while the current market price oracle for
rETH is acceptable today, an exchange-rate–based oracle may be more robust
should liquidity conditions deteriorate over time, and identified rETH as a
reasonable candidate for such a transition on L2s.

### **Proposal**

Update the rETH oracle on Base and OP Mainnet to reference a Chainlink
exchange-rate–based pricing mechanism rather than a market price feed. This
would involve sourcing the rETH/ETH exchange rate from Chainlink’s exchange-rate
feed and composing it with the existing ETH/USD price oracle, while maintaining
appropriate oracle safeguards and validation logic. The following Chainlink
resources are provided for reference as part of this transition:

- rETH/ETH Exchange Rate Feed (Chainlink):  
  https://data.chain.link/feeds/base/mainnet/reth-eth-exchange-rate
- Corresponding contract address:  
  https://basescan.org/address/0x1E6A29666288a310326B37d823Fe4Ea3937424D2

Additionally we seek to manage the debt on the OP Mainnet:

- This proposal calls to withdraw 2.6 ETH from WETH reserves
- Allocate 2.2 rETH to the rETH market to repay outstanding bad debt
- Allocate another .1 cbETH to the cbETH market to pay outstanding debt there as
  well.

### **Rationale**

This proposal's approach aligns with industry best practices for non-rebasing
LSTs, follows guidance from Moonwell’s risk advisors, and represents a proactive
improvement to oracle robustness without materially changing the risk profile of
the market under normal conditions.

### **Voting Options**

- **For** – Update the rETH oracle on Base and OP Mainnet to use a Chainlink
  exchange-rate–based feed
- **Against** – Keep the current Chainlink market price–based oracle
- **Abstain** – No preference
